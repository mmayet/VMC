How Can I?

Written after getting musalsal ḥadīth of shaking hands and interlocking hands

How can I wash my hand that has touched you ﷺ?
But I do, raising my hands, asking that I see you ﷺ.

My mouth that utters your sweet concise speech ﷺ?
But I do, dreaming of the day I get to speak to you ﷺ.

My nose that breathes the same air you breathed ﷺ?
But I do, imagining the day I get to smell you ﷺ.

My face that smiles at the mention of your blessed name ﷺ?
But I do, gitty over the day I get to be kissed by you ﷺ.

My arm that grazes by your perfect grave ﷺ?
But I do, longing for the day I get to be hugged by you ﷺ.

My head that can't stop thinking about you ﷺ?
But I do, awaiting the day I will be thought about by you ﷺ.

My ears that hear your gentle words and beautiful praises ﷺ?
But I do, yearning for the day I get to hear you ﷺ.

And my feet that tread the same earth you tread ﷺ?
But I do, as I live my life running to meet you ﷺ.

Yā Qamarūn Yā Jamīl

An short excerpt from said poem

Yā Qamarūn, Yā Jamīl,
You take my heart away,
And teach me how to feel.
How to live my life,
And get to God's light.

Your way is true,
This world is a lie,
Your life is a standard
And the Lord testified.

Your worship— perfect and serene
Divinely crafted as a model to be seen
Your eyes would tear, and your feet would swell
Crying for us, that we don't go to Hell
How can I thank you, I can't do enough
So I ask God to bless you, and in Him I put my trust.

Yā Qamarūn, Yā Jamīl,
You take my heart away,
And teach me how to feel.
How to live my life,
And get to God's light.

You ﷺ Are

Is your radiance like a lamp ﷺ?
Or the sun?
Your face, the moon— the full moon ﷺ?
Your neck, a statue of pure silver ﷺ?
Your skin, silk ﷺ?
Or rose petals?
Or brocade?
Your words, threaded pearls ﷺ?
Your teeth, hailstones ﷺ?
Or a flash of lighting?
Your sweat, musk ﷺ?
Your sweat, pearls ﷺ?
Your very being, light upon light, ﷺ?

How can you, ﷺ, be compared to such creations, when you, ﷺ, are— no doubt— greater than them all?

Saying the sun is a reflection of you, ﷺ, makes more sense;
That the grains of sand of Madīnah shine because of you, ﷺ, makes more sense.

When you, ﷺ, are God ﷻ's Beloved;
His ﷻ's Chosen One;
His ﷻ's Selected One;
His ﷻ's Pleasing One;
How can you, ﷺ, be compared to other mere creation?

God ﷻ has made the earth pure for you ﷺ;
God ﷻ has forgiven your past and future ﷺ;
God ﷻ has given you al-Kawthar, ﷺ;
God ﷻ has given you Intercession, ﷺ;
God ﷻ has placed your name, ﷺ, with His, ﷻ.

You, ﷺ, are the best of creation.
Better than land;
Better than sea;
Better than the skies;
Better than the planets;
Better than the stars;
Better than Madīnah;
Better than Makkah;
Better than The Kaʿbah;
Better than The Universe;
Better than Jannah;
Better than The Angels;
Better than Jibrīl;
Better than The Very Throne of The Almighty God, ﷻ;

—You, ﷺ, are: The Greatest Creation Of God, ﷻ.
